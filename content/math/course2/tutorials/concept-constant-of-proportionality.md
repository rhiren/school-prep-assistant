# Constant of Proportionality

## 1. What is this concept?

The constant of proportionality is the number that tells how one quantity is multiplied to get another quantity in a proportional relationship.

If `y = kx`, then `k` is the constant of proportionality.

## 2. What does it mean?

It means every output is the same multiple of the input.

For example, if `y = 3x`, then the constant of proportionality is `3` because every `y` value is 3 times the matching `x` value.

## 3. Key Ideas

- The constant of proportionality is often written as `k`.
- In a table, find it by dividing `y` by `x` when `x` is not `0`.
- In an equation, it is the coefficient of `x` in `y = kx`.
- In a graph, it tells the slope of a proportional line through the origin.
- In a word problem, it is often the unit rate.

## 4. How to Solve (Step-by-Step)

1. Check that the relationship is proportional.
2. Pick any nonzero pair of values.
3. Divide output by input.
4. Simplify the value of `k`.
5. Check that the same value works for the other pairs.

## 5. Why This Works

In a proportional relationship, the ratio between the two quantities is constant.

That shared ratio is exactly the constant of proportionality, so once you find it, you can describe the whole relationship.

## 6. Common Mistakes

- Dividing in the wrong order.
- Using a pair where the input is 0.
- Confusing a constant difference with a constant ratio.
- Forgetting to check whether the relationship is proportional first.
- Mixing up the constant of proportionality with one of the actual values in the table.

## 7. Examples (CRITICAL)

### Example 1 (easy)

Table:

- `x = 2, y = 10`
- `x = 4, y = 20`

Find `k`:
`10 / 2 = 5`

Check another row:
`20 / 4 = 5`

Answer:
The constant of proportionality is `5`.

### Example 2 (medium)

Equation: `y = 1.25x`

Answer:
The constant of proportionality is `1.25` because it is the number multiplying `x`.

### Example 3 (tricky)

A student looks at the table

- `x = 3, y = 11`
- `x = 6, y = 22`

and says the constant of proportionality is `8` because `11 - 3 = 8`.

That is not correct.
You must divide:
`11 / 3` and `22 / 6`.

Answer:
The constant of proportionality is `11/3`, not `8`.

## 8. Real-Life Connection

If apples cost `$2.50` each, then total cost is proportional to the number of apples.

The constant of proportionality is `2.50` dollars per apple.

## 9. Quick Practice

1. Easy: What is `k` in `y = 9x`?
2. Medium: In the table `(4, 14), (8, 28)`, what is `k`?
3. Slightly tricky: Why should you divide `y` by `x` instead of subtracting?

## 10. Answers to Quick Practice

1. `9`
2. `3.5`
3. Because the constant of proportionality is a multiplicative relationship, not an additive one.
