# Compare Integers

## 1. What is this concept?

Comparing integers means deciding which number is greater, smaller, or equal. Integers can be positive, negative, or zero.

This shows up in real life with temperatures, elevations, and money. For example, `-2` degrees is colder than `3` degrees.

## 2. What does it mean?

It means you are deciding where numbers are on the number line.

Numbers farther to the right are greater.
Numbers farther to the left are smaller.

## 3. Key Ideas

- Positive numbers are greater than negative numbers.
- Zero is greater than any negative number.
- Among negative numbers, the one closer to zero is greater.
- A number line can help you compare quickly.

## 4. How to Solve (Step-by-Step)

1. Identify the two integers.
2. Think about where they are on the number line.
3. Decide which one is farther right.
4. Use the correct symbol: `<`, `>`, or `=`.
5. Check whether the comparison makes sense.

## 5. Why This Works

The number line is like a path.

As you move right, numbers get larger. As you move left, numbers get smaller. Negative numbers are left of zero, so they are less than positive numbers.

## 6. Common Mistakes

- Thinking the number with the larger digits is always greater.
  For example, `-10` is not greater than `-2`.

- Forgetting that all positive numbers are greater than all negative numbers.

- Mixing up which negative number is larger.
  `-3` is greater than `-8` because it is closer to zero.

- Ignoring zero.
  Zero is between positive and negative numbers.

## 7. Examples (CRITICAL)

### Example 1 (easy)

Compare `4` and `-1`

Step 1: `4` is positive.
Step 2: `-1` is negative.
Step 3: Any positive number is greater than any negative number.

Final answer:
`4 > -1`

### Example 2 (medium)

Compare `-3` and `-7`

Step 1: Both numbers are negative.
Step 2: Find which one is closer to zero.
Step 3: `-3` is closer to zero than `-7`.

Final answer:
`-3 > -7`

### Example 3 (tricky)

A student says `-12 > -5` because 12 is greater than 5.

What is the mistake?

Step 1: Both numbers are negative.
Step 2: On the number line, `-12` is farther left than `-5`.
Step 3: Farther left means smaller.

Final answer:
The student forgot that with negative numbers, the number farther from zero is actually smaller.

## 8. Real-Life Connection

Temperature is a great example.

If one city is `-4` degrees and another city is `2` degrees, the city at `2` degrees is warmer because `2 > -4`.

## 9. Quick Practice

1. Easy: Compare `6` and `-3`
2. Medium: Compare `-2` and `-9`
3. Slightly tricky: Compare `0` and `-1`

## 10. Answers to Quick Practice

1. `6 > -3`
2. `-2 > -9`
3. `0 > -1`
