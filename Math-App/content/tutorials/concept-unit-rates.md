# Unit Rates

## 1. What is this concept?

A unit rate compares an amount to 1 unit of something else. It helps you answer questions like "how much for one item?" or "how far in one hour?"

Unit rates show up in real life all the time. You use them when you compare prices, speed, recipes, and how fast something happens.

What does a ratio mean here? A unit rate starts as a ratio, but then you rewrite it so one side becomes `1`. For example, if 3 notebooks cost 12 dollars, the ratio is 3 notebooks to 12 dollars, and the unit rate tells the cost of 1 notebook.

## 2. Key Idea

- A unit rate means the second quantity is `1`.
- To find a unit rate, you usually divide.
- Pay attention to the order of the words, like miles per hour or dollars per item.

Simple picture idea:
Think of 12 dollars shared equally into 3 boxes.
If each box gets the same amount, each box gets 4 dollars.
That is why dividing helps you find the amount for 1 box, 1 hour, or 1 item.

## 3. How to Solve

1. Find the two quantities being compared.
2. Decide what kind of rate it is, like cost per item, distance per hour, or pages per minute.
3. Decide which quantity should become `1`.
4. Divide to find the amount for `1` unit.
5. Label your answer with the correct meaning, like `miles per hour` or `dollars per notebook`.

Why this works:
If 3 notebooks cost 12 dollars, then each notebook must get an equal share of the total cost. Dividing 12 by 3 tells the cost of 1 notebook.

## 4. Common Mistakes

- Multiplying instead of dividing when the problem asks for the amount for `1`.
- Reversing the rate, like finding hours per mile when the question asks for miles per hour.
- Forgetting the units and writing only a number.
- Dividing the wrong way because the words in the question were not read carefully.

## 5. Examples

### Example 1 (easy)

4 notebooks cost 8 dollars. What is the cost of 1 notebook?

This is a real-life shopping example because unit rates help you compare prices.

Step 1: Find the total cost and total number of notebooks.
Cost = 8 dollars
Notebooks = 4

Step 2: Divide to find the amount for 1 notebook.
`8 ÷ 4 = 2`

Step 3: Add the meaning.
Each notebook costs 2 dollars.

Answer: `2 dollars per notebook`

### Example 2 (medium)

A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours. What is the speed in miles per hour?

Step 1: Find what is being compared.
Miles = 180
Hours = 3

Step 2: The question asks for miles per 1 hour.
So divide miles by hours.

`180 ÷ 3 = 60`

Step 3: Add the meaning.
The car travels 60 miles in 1 hour.

Answer: `60 miles per hour`

### Example 3 (slightly tricky)

A student says that 24 miles in 4 hours means 96 miles per hour because `24 × 4 = 96`.

What is the mistake?

Step 1: Think about the question.
Miles per hour means the amount for `1` hour.

Step 2: Use division, not multiplication.
`24 ÷ 4 = 6`

Step 3: Explain the trap.
Multiplying makes the number bigger, but it does not tell how many miles happen in just 1 hour.

Why this trap happens:
Students sometimes notice two numbers and multiply automatically. But "per 1" means split or divide to find one equal part.

Answer:
The student should divide. The correct unit rate is `6 miles per hour`.

## 6. Quick Practice

1. 5 pencils cost 10 dollars. What is the cost of 1 pencil?
2. A runner goes 18 miles in 3 hours. What is the speed in miles per hour?
3. A 12-ounce bottle of juice costs 3 dollars. What is the cost per ounce?

Answers:
1. `2 dollars per pencil`
2. `6 miles per hour`
3. `0.25 dollars per ounce`
