# Integer Operations

## 1. What is this concept?

Integer operations mean adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing positive and negative numbers. Integers include numbers like `-5`, `0`, and `8`.

You see integers in real life with temperatures, money gained or lost, and floors above or below ground.

## 2. What does it mean?

It means you are working with whole numbers that can be positive or negative.

For example:
If the temperature goes from `3` degrees to `-2` degrees, the number line helps show the change.

## 3. Key Ideas

- Positive numbers are above zero, and negative numbers are below zero.
- Adding and subtracting integers can be understood on a number line.
- Multiplying or dividing two numbers with the same sign gives a positive answer.
- Multiplying or dividing numbers with different signs gives a negative answer.

## 4. How to Solve (Step-by-Step)

1. Identify the operation.
2. Decide whether the numbers are positive or negative.
3. Use the correct rule for the operation.
4. Solve carefully.
5. Check the sign and whether the answer makes sense.

## 5. Why This Works

Think of a number line like a hallway.

Moving right means going up. Moving left means going down. Integer rules help you keep track of direction and distance.

## 6. Common Mistakes

- Forgetting the sign.
  The sign changes the meaning of the answer.

- Treating subtraction and negative signs as the same thing.
  They are related, but they do not always mean the same action.

- Mixing up the multiplication sign rules.
  Same signs give a positive. Different signs give a negative.

- Ignoring zero.
  Zero is neither positive nor negative.

## 7. Examples (CRITICAL)

### Example 1 (easy)

Solve `-3 + 5`

Step 1: Start at `-3` on the number line.
Step 2: Move 5 spaces right.
Step 3: You land on `2`.

Final answer:
`2`

### Example 2 (medium)

Solve `6 - (-4)`

Step 1: Subtracting a negative means add.
Step 2: `6 + 4 = 10`

Final answer:
`10`

### Example 3 (tricky)

Solve `-3 x 4`

Step 1: The signs are different.
Step 2: Different signs give a negative answer.
Step 3: `3 x 4 = 12`

Final answer:
`-12`

## 8. Real-Life Connection

Money is a good example.

If you owe 5 dollars, that can be shown as `-5`. If you earn 8 dollars, your total changes by adding `8`.
