# Genetics, Reproduction, and Behavior

This unit brings together three connected science ideas:

- how traits are inherited
- how organisms reproduce
- how behaviors help organisms survive and protect offspring

## Genetics

An organism's **genotype** is its set of alleles, while its **phenotype** is the trait you can observe. A dominant allele can show its trait when only one copy is present. A recessive trait appears only when both alleles are recessive.

- **Homozygous** means the two alleles are the same.
- **Heterozygous** means the two alleles are different.

Punnett squares help predict the probability of offspring traits by showing which allele combinations are possible.

## Reproduction

**Sexual reproduction** combines genetic material from two parents. It produces variation, which can help populations survive changes in the environment.

**Asexual reproduction** uses one parent and usually produces offspring genetically similar to the parent. It can happen quickly, but there is less genetic variation.

Common asexual reproduction types:

- **Binary fission**: one cell splits into two new cells.
- **Budding**: a smaller organism grows from the parent and separates.
- **Regeneration**: a new organism grows from a body part in some species.
- **Vegetative reproduction**: new plants grow from roots, stems, or leaves.

Plants can also reproduce with seeds. Seeds are spread by wind, water, animals, or bursting seed pods.

## Behavior

An **instinct** is an inherited behavior present without being taught. A **learned behavior** develops through experience.

Common learned behaviors:

- **Habituation**: ignoring a repeated harmless stimulus
- **Observational learning**: learning by watching another organism
- **Conditioning**: linking an action or signal with a result
- **Playing**: practicing skills through safe exploration

Some animal behaviors also help protect offspring. Examples include guarding nests, carrying babies, feeding young, or using courtship behaviors to attract a mate and increase the chance of reproduction.
